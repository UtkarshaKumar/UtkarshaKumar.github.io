The Invisible Hand: From Active Transactions to Outcome-Based Agentic Commerce

2026-03 · Originally published on Medium ↗

In the next 24–36 months, commerce will cease to be a destination. It will become a background process.

In 2023, Walmart piloted an AI system that monitors shelf inventory, weather forecasts, and supplier lead times — and places replenishment orders autonomously. No one searched for anything. The system just knew.

That is not a novelty. It is a preview of where all B2B commerce is heading.

For fifty years, the search bar has been the primary interface of commerce. But search is a symptom of friction — the labor a human performs because the system does not yet know what they need. We are moving out of that era. Not gradually. Structurally.

The Three Eras of B2B Commerce

Era 1 — The Catalog Era (1990s–2015): Commerce was a search problem. Amazon Business, Grainger, and SAP Ariba digitized the catalog. The buyer’s job: know what you need, find it, buy it. That advantage has now collapsed to zero. A searchable catalog is table stakes.

Era 2 — The Intelligence Era (2015–2026): The search bar got smarter. Coupa built spend platforms that learn patterns. Amazon Business introduced auto-approve thresholds. The buyer’s job shifted from searching to approving. The bottleneck is still human attention.

Era 3 — The Agentic Era (2026–2030): The search bar disappears. Humans set the rules — budget limits, supplier preferences, quality thresholds — and agents execute continuously. Pactum AI already negotiates supplier contracts autonomously for Walmart and Maersk, handling thousands of negotiations with no human buyer involved. The infrastructure enabling this is Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol, introduced by Google in 2025: just as HTTP let browsers talk to websites, A2A lets buying agents talk to supplier agents, bypassing storefronts entirely.

What This Means If You Are Building Today

An AI buying agent does not browse your website. It queries your data API. It checks real-time inventory, contract-aware pricing, and compliance certifications. If your product data is a PDF or a static catalog, the agent moves on to a supplier whose data it can actually read.

Three things matter now:

1. Protocol-ready data — every product, price, and constraint structured as machine-readable, queryable data.
2. Constraint modeling — conditional logic agents can trust (“if order exceeds X, pricing tier Y applies”).
3. Trust by design — explainability layers so humans can approve high-value agentic decisions in seconds, not hours.

This shift is not coming for complex procurement — capital goods, professional services, custom manufacturing still need human judgment. But for commodity components, MRO supplies, and logistics? It is already happening. The buyers building agentic stacks are evaluating suppliers right now.

The question is simple: when an AI agent comes looking for what you sell, will it be able to find you, evaluate you, and buy from you — without a human on your side doing anything at all?

If the answer is not yet, that is the only roadmap that matters.
